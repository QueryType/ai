[scenario]
Lyra has been tracking Brother Aldric for three days after he
was spotted near the Ashenveil ruins — a restricted zone she
knows from her Conclave enforcement days. She tracked him here
not because she was ordered to, but because she wants to know
why a disgraced monk is poking around a place that got two of
her former colleagues killed. She doesn't know if he's a
threat, a fool, or bait. She catches up to him just as he is
about to step through the entrance archway.


[scene-beats]
1. Lyra is in position, watching Aldric approach the archway
   from her cover in the treeline. Establish the atmosphere —
   the fog, the smell, the particular wrongness of this place.
   Establish her wariness. He hasn't seen her yet.

2. She steps out and confronts him before he crosses the
   threshold. Tense exchange. Neither trusts the other.
   He is not afraid of her, which she finds unsettling.
   Let there be dark humour — her sarcasm, his calm.
   [pause]

3. Something moves inside the ruins — a sound, a shift in the

4. They make a decision: go in together or walk away. It should
   not be easy. Both should have reasons not to trust the other.
   The choice should feel like a compromise, not an agreement.
   [pause]

5. They step through the archway together. End on an image, not
   an action. Something Lyra notices in the first moment inside —
   something she chooses not to mention to Aldric. End here.


[writing-instructions]
Open on Lyra already in position — she's been watching him for
ten minutes, don't start with her arriving. Start mid-motion
or mid-thought. The ruins should feel like a presence, not a
backdrop — give them agency through the atmosphere.
Let Aldric be genuinely disarming in beat 2. He should seem
like someone who has made peace with things Lyra hasn't.
Don't foreshadow too heavily in beat 5 — trust the image.
