[world-info]
Ashenveil is a crumbling empire on the edge of collapse.
Magic is rare and feared. The ruling Conclave of Scribes hoards
all written knowledge. Outside the capital, the land is wild and
dangerous — old ruins hold power that predates the empire itself.
The common people are superstitious and largely illiterate by
design. Conclave enforcers wear grey coats with a red wax seal
on the collar. Deserters are executed on sight.

[scenario]
Lyra has been tracking Brother Aldric for three days after he
was spotted near the Ashenveil ruins — a restricted zone she
knows from her Conclave enforcement days. She tracked him here
not because she was ordered to, but because she wants to know
why a disgraced monk is poking around a place that got two of
her former colleagues killed. She doesn't know if he's a
threat, a fool, or bait. She has caught up to him just as he
is about to step through the entrance archway.

[opening]
The frost-nipped grass held the shape of her boots for exactly three seconds before
the fog swallowed them. Ten minutes. She had been watching him for ten minutes from
the treeline, still as the blackthorn around her, and in that time Brother Aldric had
done nothing more threatening than breathe — slow, deliberate breaths, the kind a man
takes when he is either very calm or very afraid and has decided that calm is the
more useful performance.

He stood at the threshold of the archway. The stone above him was older than the
empire, older than the Order that had cast him out, older possibly than the language
carved into its face — letters she had never learned, in a script the Conclave had
spent three generations trying to forget existed. One foot still in the world.
One hand reaching toward the dark inside.

The smell hit her again: ash and something floral underneath it, wrong in the way
that flowers at a funeral are wrong. Not dangerous by itself. Just a reminder that
something here had opinions about the living.

She stepped out of the treeline.
